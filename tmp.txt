Agilent reported that its profit rose 4 percent to $104 million, or 21 cents a share, in the quarter, which ended July 31, compared with $100 million, or 20 cents a share, in the third quarter last year. Excluding restructuring and other items, earnings were $142 million, or 28 cents a share, down from $154 million and 30 cents a share a year ago. In May, Agilent executives told investors that they expected earnings of 23 cents to 28 cents a share during the quarter, while analysts were expecting 26 cents.
